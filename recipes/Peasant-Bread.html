Peasant Bread

Description

This is a lovely recipe from Alexandra's Kitchen. This recipe has been in her family for 45 years. It is quick and easy for all who crave a crusty buttery roll in under 3 hours. This recipe will take you from start to finish with minimal sticky hands and no kneading.

I will guide you through a vegan alternative to this recipe. So roll up your sleeves and set out your favorite butter alternative. It's time to begin our journey to the easiest rolls you will ever make.

origin author: Alexandra Stafford

total time: 2 hours 27 minutes

yield: 12 dozen rolls

Ingredients

  • 512 g unbleached all-purpose or bread flour
  • 10 g kosher salt
  • 454 g lukewarm water (made by mixing 1.5 cups cold water with 0.5 cup boiling water)
  • 8 g sugar
  • 8 g instant yeast
  • room temperature vegan butter of choice, about 2 tablespoons

Steps

  1. Mixing the dough:In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast. Add the water. Mix until the flour is absorbed.
  2. First rise: Cover bowl with plastic wrap and set aside in a warm spot to rise for at least an hour. (In the winter or if you are letting the bread rise in a cool place, it might take as long as two hours to rise.)
  3. Preheat: Preheat the oven to 218c. If using a convection oven, preheat to 208c. Grease a 12 cup muffin tin with about two tablespoons of butter. Using two forks, punch down your dough, scraping it from the sides of the bowl, which it will be clinging to. As you scrape it down try to pull the dough toward the center. You want to loosen the dough entirely from the sides of the bowl, and you want to make sure you’ve punched it down. Then, take your two forks and divide the dough equal into the greased muffin tin.
  4. Second rise: Let the dough rise again for about 20 to 30 minutes on the countertop near the oven (or near a warm spot) or until it has risen to just below or above the top of the bowls.
  5. Bake it: Bake for 15 minutes. Reduce the heat to 190c (180c for convection oven) and bake for 15 to 17 minutes longer. Remove from the oven and turn the loaves onto cooling racks. If you’ve greased the bowls well, the loaves should fall right out onto the cooling racks. If the loaves look a little pale and soft when you’ve turned them out onto your cooling racks, place the loaves into the oven (outside of their bowls) and let them bake for about 5 minutes longer. Remove from the oven and let cool for 10 minutes before cutting.
